samuel benson & charlotte Walker from Co. Meath Ireland

 

What I think!

Summerhill Demesne  Co. Meath Ireland   Photograph by © MBrace July 2005

 

Samuel Benson was a successful Protestant farmer. He made enough money in Ireland to bring 7 adult children to Australia and establish a household which was no mean feat in 19th C Ireland. He was neither the aristocracy nor the peasantry, probably a middle man. He leased land on Summerhill and collected rent from smaller tenants. When Summerhill was sold after 1836 he moved to Isaacstown Rathmolyon where he leased land from Rev Robert Fowler  for 2 lives at an annual rent of £19.55 and probably sublet to  tenant farmers. He was married in Dublin and some of his children were baptised at St. Catherine’s in Dublin so he possibly had some status in the society of the day and family connections there as it was 25 miles from Summerhill . He was listed as a gentleman upon his death so presumably had sufficient money to sustain himself and his wife in Australia. After the Napoleonic Wars agricultural prices in Ireland slumped and unhappy tenants and labourers had formed into militant groups such as the ‘Whiteboys’ and ‘Ribbonmen’.  His home and family had been a target and he wanted to leave ‘The Troubles’ behind him.

Charlotte and Samuel lived in Brougham Street, Woolloomooloo. They also owned 2 houses and shops at 156 and 158 William Street, Darlinghurst, now Kings Cross.


 

What you should know


Samuel George Benson was born in Co. Meath Ireland in about 1787. His Death certificate recorded that he married Charlotte Walker in Dublin, Ireland in 1807. Charlotte was born in  Co. Meath Irela

protestant parish records

1802 saw Bishop Thomas Lew Augustus O'Beirne instruct his registrars to obtain from the clergy, accurate written lists of their parishioners, with particulars of the family members. The Walkers were on the list for Rathmolyon Co. Meath.

Her parents were named David and Anne Walker. Charlotte also had a brother named John born about 1783. Samuel and Charlotte sailed from Plymouth  aboard the Earl Grey with all of their family to Sydney, New South Wales, Australia  in 1841. They were sponsored by William Walker. Samuel was about 54 and Charlotte 58 when they emigrated.

They lived at 182  Brougham Street, Woolloomooloo which is just behind King’s Cross in Sydney.
