James Benson was the first chartered accountant in New South Wales. He bought a hotel in Gundagai in the 1880s because it was believed the railway was going to go through there. He lived at “Kiora”, Pine Avenue, Waverley when the family returned to Sydney from Gundagai. Later in life his son Harry went to Kalgoorlie and Koolgardie mining for gold and I’m pretty sure James went with him. He lived at “Aegra” Randwick in his old age.


valerie benson’s story (his granddaughter)

He was travelling on the ferry and he saw some small children floating in a tub in the harbour and he said, “You’d think the parents would keep a better eye on  their children  than to let them do such a thing.”

It turned out that they were his own children, Harry, Ettie and another sibling.

What you should know


james william benson

James Benson was born in Sydney in 1848 and baptised at St. James Church of England, Sydney, the second of 11 children born to James Benson and Elizabeth Hayward.








          St. James C. of E., King St. Sydney


He married Louisa McKeown on the 8 July 1876 at St. Leonards, Sydney. Louisa was the daughter of William Henry McKeown and Harriet Moran. Louisa was born in 1854 at Lane Cove on the North Shore of Sydney.

James Benson died of myelitis at Waverley House, Sydney on the 15th Jan 1913 and was buried in the C of E section of Long Bay Cemetery, Sydney. Louisa died in 1924 at the Coast Hospital, Little Bay, Sydney.
