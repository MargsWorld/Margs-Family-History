Mary Anne Taylor & James lecky

 
 
Mabel Lecky told me Mary Anne Taylor had come from a a wealthy and important family in Ramelton. There were lots of Taylor headstones in the old church yard. Her brothers were involved in the weaving industry.

Linen had its ups and downs because of competition with cotton and along with the changes bought about by industrialisation times were very hard for people in Donegal. After marriage James and Mary Ann were quite poor with one field, maybe 4 acres near the house. All of the Leckey children except John had to emigrate to survive. Mabel said when Mary Ann learnt of her daughter Sarah’s fate in Australia, which was not until well after she had been stranded, perhaps 3-6 months, she suffered a stroke and was palsied down one side. She said she should never have let Sarah go. Many said it was the worry that caused her ill health and early death. It must have been very hard for families to say goodbye to so many of their children.
