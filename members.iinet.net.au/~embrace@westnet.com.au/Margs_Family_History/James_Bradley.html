James Bradley was a First Fleet convict. He was born in Middlesex, England about 1765. It is not possible to ascertain his parentage as 2 convicts named James Bradley came out on the First Fleet.

James Bradley married Sarah Barnes in St. John’s Church, Parramatta on the 12th August 1792. She was a Third Fleet convict. They had 10 children including  the 2nd child Lucy who is our forebear.

James Bradley was granted land and farmed at the Eastern Farms, Kissing Point, now Putney.

He died on the 13th February 1838 and is buried at St. Anne’s, Ryde, N.S.W.

There were two James Bradley's convicted in the Old Bailey Court in London and sentenced to seven years transportation to Australia, one in 1784 for stealing a linen handkerchief valued at one shilling, the other in 1785 for stealing a handkerchief with a purple border valued at two shillings. They were both sentenced 7 years transportation.




The James Bradley aboard the “Alexander” reached Australia but was the to die on arrival in the new colony. His burial was the first recorded on 3 February 1788. The second James Bradley sailed aboard the Scarborough and he is the one that is our forebear. Both cases are well documented but the authors of the main reference books disagree about which one was transported on the "Scarborough".

There is no doubt that our ancestor was aboard the Scarborough. I think  he pinched the 1/- hanky  on the 6th May 1784.
