A clairvoyant lady visited us one day and described to me a woman in a floral dress tending plants in a shadehouse attached to the side of a house. When I told Mum and Dad this story expecting Mum to say it was her Mother, Dad began to cry as it was a description of his mother who had a shadehouse attached to her home which she enjoyed tending.

When Oscar was at away at war Margaret lived with her parents and made all of the clothes for her sisters. She was a very good seamstress and she passed this skill on to her daughter Marj.

Until his death Dad would cry every time we would talk about his mother. He loved her very dearly and missed her terribly when she died. He joined the Army straight after her death and went to war.

 

What you should know

Margaret Edgar was born on the 19th August 1894 at 462 Kent Street, Sydney. She married Oscar Ernest Stait in Portland, New South Wales in Jun 1916 just before Oscar sailed off to France in World War 1. Margaret and Ossie had 2 children, Marjorie Jack and John Edgar. Marjorie was born while Oscar was away at the Western Front. In 1940 Margaret died from cancer at the age of 46 in Concord West where her sister and mother were living.

At St. Stephen’s Church of England on Saturday morning, the Rev. E.A. Homfray, Rector, celebrated the marriage of Margaret, eldest daughter of Mr. J. Edgar of Lithgow, and late of Portland, with Oscar, youngest son of Mr. J. Stait of Portland. The bride, who was in full bridal costume, was given away by her father, and was accompanied by her sister, Manie, as bridesmaid. Mr J. Collier acted as best man. The party afterwards motored to Lithgow. The bridegroom has lived all his life here, and is about to leave for the front with the expeditionary forces. The happy couple received a large number of presents of a valuable and useful kind.

The Lithgow Mercury. 30th August 1916. p. 3
