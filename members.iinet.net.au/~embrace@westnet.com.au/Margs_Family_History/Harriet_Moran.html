Harriet Moran was 17 when she left Ireland with her family. The ship the Elphinstone was made to carry 100 sailors and on this trip it carried 248 immigrants. I was told that Harriet was travelling with another family to provide help with the children on the journey. It must have been a very difficult voyage and a lot would have rested on her shoulders when her mother died off Indonesia. I don’t know how Harriet coped when the family arrived in Australia but as the oldest child I’m sure a lot of responsibility would have rested with her.

Harriet was said to be a cheerful and unselfish helper to her husband and with 10 children and the task of caring for the many guests that the family opened their doors to she must have been a very busy person.

There are seldom obituaries to women such as Harriet but I think they are the quiet achievers in these stories of male success and honour.

 

What I think!

Harriet’s father Edward was born 10 Feb 1805 in Gowran Grange, Kilkenny. In 1839 he was living in Gowran, Kilkenny with his family.

He listed himself as a labourer on his immigration records. He died in Balmain on the 18 Mar 1869.

When we travelled to Kilkenny, Ireland in 2005. I had a hunch I would find some information about him. A quick trip to the library while everyone was lunching in the pub delivered me the Tithe Applotment Books which told me that he came from Ballybrazil.

His father was Maurice Moran and his mother Harriet Corcoran. Griffith’s Valuation places Maurice at Aughclare, Ballybrazil, Wexford in 1859. I had an email contact from RClark who told me that descendants of Maurice told her that Maurice was an Attorney in Ireland.

Moran : surname Origin

Moran (Irish: Ó Móráin) is a modern Irish surname and derived from membership of a medieval dynastic sept. The name means a descendent of Mórán, translated as Big One. Morans were a respected sept of the Uí Fiachrach dynasty in the western counties of Mayo and Sligo. In Ireland, where the name descended from the Gaelic, it is generally pronounced (phonetically) "more-in", an anglicized approximate of the Irish pronunciation. Elsewhere, pronunciation follows the French surname, Mo rant, anglicized to (phonetically) "more-anne".

Moran : O’Murchain, from Murchadha, ‘sea-warrior’ is now generally anglicized as Moran. The family were originally of east Offaly, near the Co. Kildare border. (Bhulb 2002.)

What you should know


Harriet Moran or henriett moren

Harriet Moran was born in Ballybrazil, Mooncoin, Kilkenny, Ireland in 1824. Her father was Edward Moran and her mother Anne Burrows. They had married in Ireland in 1823. Harriet was the eldest of 8 children. The family  travelled to Sydney aboard the “Elphinstone” from Plymouth, England in 1840. Her mother Ann Burrows died on 31 Jul 1840 at sea off Sulawesi, Indonesia. Harriet married William Henry McKeown on 5 Feb 1845 at St. Lawrence’s Anglican Church, George Street, Sydney.













They moved to Lane Cove in 1844 or 1845 and established their home and family there. Harriet gave birth to 10 children and died at Ormond St., Ashfield on the 4 Nov 1914. She was buried on the 5 Nov 1914 in the general section of Waverley Cemetery.
