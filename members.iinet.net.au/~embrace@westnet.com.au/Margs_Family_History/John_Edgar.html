John Edgar owned a bicycle shop at 133 York Street, Sydney and raced bicycles. His family lived at 462 Kent Street, Sydney and in The Rocks they then moved to Portland and then to Lithgow. He was a tool maker. He was a noted amateur cyclist in the 1890s and won many bicycle races. There is a silver medal won by John Edgar on display in Hyde Park Barracks. He was the 1 mile Champion of New South Wales and he also won the 2 mile 50 guinea Elkington Challenge Cup and the 3 mile open roadster handicap on a New Rapid cycle. He rode the fastest mile in N.S.W. on a penny farthing and raced the first bike with pneumatic tyres. Bennett and Woods Co., started off as Bennett and Edgar. He couldn't make enough money and left the company.

He worked as a toolmaker in the Lithgow small arms factory. Dad remembers him playing 500 and eating gorgonzola cheese which he really liked, especially with 'jumpers' in it. He played cards with Bunger Young who was the Manager of the Lithgow Co-op. He sang 'I belong to Glasgow' when in a singing mood. Pat Marshall says,"I too have memories of 'I belong to Glasgow'. Grandma and Grandpa lived with us for the last few years of their lives and, as a child, I remember Grandpa sitting with tears running down his cheeks, listening to Harry Lauder singing 'I belong to Glasgow’ on the wireless."

notes from john Stait, his grandson

What you should know

John Edgar was born on the 13th Nov 1866 in St. Cuthberts, Edinburgh, Scotland to Gideon Edgar and Margaret Moyes. There were 3 boys and 3 girls. Gideon was a stonemason and the two older boys became stonemasons as well. John became a toolmaker.

He arrived in Australia on the “SS Cloncurry in Brisbane in July 1885.

He met Sarah Leckey in Miller’s Point, Sydney where he boarded in the boarding house she managed. They married on the 9th March 1894 at St. Andrew’s Scots Church, Sydney. John and Sarah had 6 children, 5 girls and a boy who died shortly after birth. They left Sydney and moved to Portland and then Lithgow where they built a house on Hill Street, Lithgow. John Edgar died on the 11th January 1938 at 57 Iandra Street, Concord West.
