Doris Benson had a pretty hard childhood. Her older sister died when Doris was 3 years of age. Her father died 6 years later of delerium tremens from alcoholism. Her mother died when she was 21 and she stayed with a chemist and his wife and they were very strict. Mum told me she was roused on most severely because her petticoat was hanging and her fiancee saw it. They told her she was immoral.

She worked at David Jones as a saleswoman prior to her marriage.

After her marriage she lived at “Sherwood” Paton Street, Merrylands. She had housekeepers, gardeners and cooks to assist in the management of the home where they entertained lavishly. Some of the famous people they entertained were Nellie Melba and  Charles Kingsford Smith.

After Harry Benson’s early death all of the money that the family had had was lost and at the age of 44 she was left to raise Valerie who was 14 and Clem 9. They lived in a flat in Waverley in straitened circumstances.

After the war she lived on the farm at Burrawang with Mum and Dad. Dad was very fond of her and always said that she was a ‘real good old stick’. She died the day before Clem’s first child Elizabeth was born.
