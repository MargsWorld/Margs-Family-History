What you should know


Lucy Bradley was born at Eastern Farms at Kissing Point, New South Wales on the 24th November 1796.

She was the daughter of James Bradley (1765-1838) and Sarah Barnes (1775-1853) who were both First Fleet convicts.

Lucy married James McManus on  7th April 1814 at St. John’s Church of England, Parramatta when she was 17 years of age.

She and James had 7 children. She was the mother of Sarah McManus.

Lucy remarried Isaac Bolton on the 15th September 1845 at St. Matthews Church of England Windsor, 6 years after James had died.

She had another 2 daughters with Isaac Bolton. One of them married Samuel Marsden’s grandson. Samuel Marsden was known as the ‘flogging parson’.

Lucy died at Meadow Flat, New South Wales on 14th November 1878.

She is buried in an unmarked grave at Meadow Flat, New South Wales with James McManus and Isaac Bolton.
