Richard’s older brother John was transported to Tasmania for life and his other brother Edmond was sentenced to 14 years in Worcestershire Assizes but died while awaiting transportation. Hannah got up to a little bit more mischief but I am not sure what happened to her in life.

What you should know

Richard Stait’s  surname is variously written as Staight, Staite, Straight, Stute and there are a number of other variations of the surname found in Gloucestershire. It is a topographical surname for someone who lived by a landing-place, on the banks of a river or estuary.

Richard Stait was born in about 1812 in Tewkesbury, Gloucestershire. Richard and his brother John were baptised in Tewkesbury on the 5th February 1817.

He was the son of Lydia Bishop (1776 - 1837) and  James Staight (1777 - 2 May  1823) of Tewkesbury. James was a waterman on the Severn or Avon Rivers and died when Richard was about 11. He and his young siblings would have been thrown on their own resources at this point.

Lydia was living on Parochial Relief at 57 Eagles’ Alley Tewkesbury in 1832 and her firstborn Anne who was lame was living at  34 Eagles’ Alley. Lydia died in 1837 at the back of the Avon River.

James Staight’s parents were William Staight ( ? - 1776) and Ann Turner (1746 -  ?) of Tewkesbury.

Lydia Bishop’s parents were George Bishop (1749-1812) and Hannah Adney ( ? - 1812) also from Tewkesbury.










Richard Stait had a number of occupations listed. He was a brewer, labourer, stable hand and  sawyer at various time during his life but mostly he was a convict.

Richard was sent to the prison hulk Justitia in Woolwich after his sentencing on 21st January 1831and before his transportation on the Parmelia to Australia in 1832.

He was sentenced at the Gloucester Assizes to 7 years transportation for stealing at a public house.

Upon arrival in Australia he was consigned to Hyde Park Barracks where he was held  for the rest of his sentence. On the 15th January 1839 he was granted his Certificate of Freedom and he married Sarah McManus on the 29th March 1841 at St. Lawrence’s Church of England in Sydney.

Sarah and Richard had 10 children, 4 girls and 6 boys. Emma the 1st born child born in 1835 before they married.

Richard fell from a horse and died at Woodside near Bathurst on the 16th July 1857. He is buried at Kelso Holy Trinity Church. He was 45 years of age. His youngest child was only 7 months old.
